About the Yorkshire & Humber Neonatal Operations Delivery Network (ODN)

The Yorkshire and Humber neonatal network is made up of 20 different hospitals and covers a geographical area ranging from York to Chesterfield (north to south) and Grimsby to the Pennines (east to west).

We also have a designated transport team – Embrace – who transfer babies by ambulance, helicopter or occasionally by aeroplane.

Within our network we have four hospitals which provide the majority of the intensive care which is required in our area – Bradford Royal Infirmary, Hull Royal Infirmary, Jessop Wing (Sheffield) and Leeds General Infirmary. Many other hospitals provide high dependency care and a few (Bassetlaw, Harrogate and Scarborough) focus on special care. Whichever hospital your baby is in, you can be assured that they are there for a very good reason and every effort will be made to keep baby as near to home as possible while still receiving the treatment they require.
