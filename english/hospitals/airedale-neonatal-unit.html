Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
During office hours access to the unit is through the children’s outpatient clinic. At the weekend and evenings, follow the signs for ‘Ward 21 – Maternity’ and the unit is just down the corridor on level 2. There is a lift at the maternity entrance which is accessible at all times.

Nearest car park...
There are several pay and display car parks surrounding the hospital and parents visiting the unit can have a free parking permit which is obtained from nursing staff on the unit. Grandparents can purchase a weekly reduced cost permit from Car Park Kiosk, ask staff for details.

Car parking charges...

  • Up to 2 hours - £2.50
  • 2-4 hours - £3.00
  • Up to 24 hours - £3.50

Nearest bus stop...
The No 25 (Keighley to Burnley and vice versa) stops at several locations on the perimeter road, the nearest bus stop is the West Car Park and then goes out onto the main road above the hospital (Skipton Road) where there are stops at either side of the road by the entrance to the hospital.

Nearest railway station...
Steeton Station (on the Airedale line which runs between Skipton and Keighley) is a fifteen minute walk from the hospital

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents are allowed to visit 24 hours a day.

Number of visitors...
Maximum of two.

Siblings...
No siblings under the age of two are allowed to visit. There is a play area and computer for children above this age to use.

Grandparents...
Grandparents are allowed to visit between the hours of 14:00-20:00.

Family and friends...
Unfortunately other family members (apart from grandparents) and friends are not allowed to visit the unit but this policy is currently under review.

Restrictions...
Only parents, grandparents and siblings (over the age of two) are allowed to visit. Anyone feeling unwell with colds etc. are asked not to visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Visitors are asked to use the hand gel on entry and exit from the unit. They are also asked to wash their hands when inside the nursery and hand gel can also be used inside the nursery. Visitors are also asked to remove their outdoor coats prior to entering the nursery.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones may be used in the parent’s rooms but not in the nurseries. Phones must be switched to ‘silent’ when on the unit.

Parent facilities...
There is a day room with a microwave, tea making facilities (tea and coffee are provided), a TV and internet access together with magazines, weekly local papers, a selection of takeaway menus and information leaflets. There is also a quiet room for private discussions.

Parent accommodation...
The unit has two rooms for families to use prior to discharge or, in the case of an emergency. There is also a day room with a microwave, tea making facilities, a TV and internet access.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
Breast feeding support is offered by several members of neonatal staff who have undergone further BFI training. Nurses can support parent’s feeding choices.   There are breast pumps available for you to express milk on the unit by the cot side or in private.  Privacy screens are provided.  Breast pumps can also be loaned out once you are discharged home and your baby is still resident on the Unit.

Facilities for siblings...
There is a play area for pre-school age children and books, TV and internet is available for older siblings.

Parent support group...
The Bliss representative visits each week and contact information is on display in the parent’s kitchen and main corridor. There is a local Bliss support group

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a prayer room / church on level 1 ground floor (orange signs) behind the main entrance and multi-faith support can be contacted through switchboard, 01535 652511.

Play specialists...
Play specialists can be accessed from the children’s ward. Long term babies will be visited and toys will be provided.

Telephone and computer access...
The unit has free WiFi and there is a computer located in the day room that parents can use. Direct unit telephone numbers are given out on admission. Our direct line number is 01535 292391.

Bereavement support...
An onsite bereavement officer is available when required and spiritual support is also available when required. This can be arranged through nursing staff on the unit.

Counselling...
Counselling can be accessed if required, please ask a member of the nursing staff.

Other important stuff...
Airedale Hospital is a level 2 unit with capacity to care for twelve babies. On admission, parents will be given and information booklet which gives full details of the unit together with a photograph of their baby. The unit can also loan breast pumps to breastfeeding mothers.

What to expect

Ward round...
There are daily ward rounds which take place each morning.

Baby rest time...
We do not have a designated rest time. Each baby has their individual needs and care is planned accordingly. There are daily ward rounds which take place each morning

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Parents can talk to the member of staff looking after their baby on that day or baby’s named nurse. If neither of these people are available the nurse in charge will be happy to speak to them.

Talking to the medical team...
The medical team talk to parents on admission, during the ward round and on discharge. If parents need to speak to their baby’s consultant, arrangements can be made.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns please talk to the nurse caring for your baby or the nurse in charge. If you are unsure of names and job titles, there is a photoboard to help you located on the unit main corridor.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
There is a PALS Office, which offers free and confidential advice to parents, situated on Level 1 at the Entrance to Ward 18.  PALS Information Leaflets and Posters are situated in the Parent Areas on the Unit.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is a dining room on the first floor which is open 07:30-18:00 on Monday to Friday and 07:30-15:00 on Saturday and Sunday. There are also two shops which sell sandwiches, hot drinks, sweets etc. and these are located in the outpatient area and near the main reception.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a Morrisons local store a short walk from the hospital and several vending machines on site which sell drinks, snacks, sweets.

Cashpoint machine...
There is a cashpoint machine on the main corridor in the hospital, level 2 purple (sign above main entrance).

Access to a midwife...
There is a maternity assessment unit on site which is open Monday to Friday 08:30-16:30 and staff can arrange an appointment if necessary.

Interpreting services...
A telephone interpreting service is available for most languages and a face-to-face interpreter can be arranged via ward staff.

Laundry facilities...
Baby clothes are washed on the Unit. There are no laundry facilities for parents.
