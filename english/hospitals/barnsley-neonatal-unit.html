Barnsley Hospital

Gawber Road, Pogmoor, Barnsley S75 2EP
Hospital Switchboard: 01226 730000
Unit Telephone Number: 01226 432269

How to access the unit...

The neonatal unit is located on level 3 in the Purple Zone. Access is gained by pressing the buzzer and a member of staff will let you in.

Nearest Car Park...

We have 632 public parking spaces in total, 343 within the immediate grounds of the hospital, and 289 spaces in a car park on Pogmoor Road (postcode for SatNav S75 2EP).

Car Parking charges...

Parking charges are:

  • 0-15 minutes FREE (except in the Helensburgh Close car park where there is no free period)
  • 0-1 Hour - £1.30
  • 1-2 Hours - £2.80
  • 2-4 Hours - £4.10
  • 4-24 Hours - £6.90
  • Weekly charge £10.00

Parents of babies on the neonatal unit are entitled to a free parking pass - please ask a member of staff for a form.

Nearest Bus Stop...

Barnsley Hospital is extremely well served by buses in and around the borough. Buses run almost every five minutes from the town centre Monday to Sunday. Public transport services are provided by a number of companies throughout the Barnsley District. To plan your journey use the Travel South Yorkshire journey planner.

Nearest railway station...

The nearest railway station is Barnsley which has a regular service from Huddersfield, Leeds and Sheffield. From Barnsley Transport Interchange, you will probably find it easier to catch a bus or taxi to the hospital - to plan your journey use the Travel South Yorkshire journey planner.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents are allowed to visit 24 hours a day.

Number of visitors...
The maximum number of visitors is three to a cot. Any other visitors must wait outside the unit.

Siblings...
Siblings can visit but they must be accompanied by a parent.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 08:00-20:00.

Family and friends...
Family and friends can visit between 14:00-20:00 however, they must be accompanied by one of the parents or by prior arrangement.

Restrictions...
Visitors under the age of 16 (except siblings) are not allowed.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Please gel your hands before entering the unit and then wash your hands before going to your baby.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones are not allowed on the unit but can be used in the lobby.

Parent facilities...
Bliss champions visit every Thursday and at the weekend.

Parent accommodation...
We have two bedrooms for rooming in prior to your baby going home.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
We have a room which is used for expressing breast milk.  There are breast pumps and breast feeding support workers available while you are on the unit.

Facilities for siblings...
We have a playroom with a small selection of toys.

Parent support group...
Bliss champions visit every Tuesday, Thursday and Sunday.

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a chapel on site, and parents can have access to a chaplain (CofE, Roman Catholic, SM and Free Church) or Imam. The contact number is 01226 432725, or ask a member of the nursing team. The hospital conducts a Sunday service (Church of England) at 10:45am. Volunteers are available to bring patients to the service and then return them to the wards. For more information contact Father Peter Needham on 01226 432725.

Play specialists...
We have a dedicated play team available. Please ask a member of the nursing staff to access the play team.

Telephone and computer access...
There is a payphone in the main entrance to the hospital but no public WiFi.

Bereavement support...
There is a bereavement midwife which the nursing staff will contact on your behalf if required.

Counselling...
Unfortunately this service is not available at Barnsley General Hospital.

What to expect

Ward round...
A ward round takes place every morning at 09:00.

Baby rest time...
Baby’s rest time is between 14:00-15:00.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please talk to the nurse allocated to look after your baby.

Talking to the medical team...
Please ask a member of the nursing team who will arrange for you to meet with your baby’s named consultant for an update.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please talk to the nurse looking after your baby, or the Lead Nurse.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS service is located in the main entrance to the hospital or you can call them on 01226 432330 (email barnsleypals@nhs.net). The PALS service operates from 09:00-16:00 (Monday, Tuesday and Thursday) and 09:00-12:30 (Wednesday and Friday) - excluding Bank Holidays

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is a restaurant located on the first floor of the main hospital, and a café is in the outpatients department.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a WH Smith news agent, based near the main entrance of the Hospital, which stocks newspapers, magazines, snacks, confectionery, drinks and toiletry essentials. This store is open seven days a week.

Cashpoint machine...
There is a cashpoint machine located in the main entrance to the hospital which is free of charge.

Access to a midwife...
If this facility is needed – ask a member of the neonatal team who will arrange for you to be seen.

Interpreting services...
Interpreting services are available by telephone and in person and can be arranged by the nursing team.

Laundry facilities...
Baby clothes and bedding is washed on the neonatal unit, your own baby clothes should be washed by you at home. There are no laundry facilities for parents.
