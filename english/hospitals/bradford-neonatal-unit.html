How to access the unit...

The neonatal unit is best accessed via gate 5 off Smith Lane (use postcode BD9 6DG). Once in the reception of the Women’s & Newborn building turn left and the unit is easy to find.

Nearest Car Park...

The nearest visitors’ car park is located next to the Women’s & Newborn building- Gate 5, off Smith Lane (for Gate 5 Smith Lane use postcode BD9 6DG).

Car Parking charges...

Parking charges are:

  • Up to 2 hours - £2.50
  • Up to 3 hours - £3.50
  • Up to 4 hours - £4.50
  • Up to 5 hours - £5.50
  • 5 - 10 hours - £8

Parking permits are available for parents, please ask the nurse caring for your baby for a form. The cost of the permit is £32 monthly or £8.26 per week.

Nearest Bus Stop...

There are two bus stops close to the neonatal unit. One on Smith Lane outside of the maternity unit (bus number 620) and one on Duckworth Lane outside of the main hospital entrance (bus numbers 617/618). All buses travel into the city centre.

Nearest railway station...

Bradford city centre has 2 stations: Bradford Interchange and Bradford Forster square, these are a short bus or taxi ride from the hospital.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents are welcome on the unit at any time of the day or night.

Number of visitors...
Due to limited space and our commitment to maintaining a quiet restful environment we allow two visitors per cot at any one time (including parents). There is a family sitting area where friends and family can wait.

Siblings...
Your other children are welcome on the neonatal unit until 21:00 each day.

Grandparents...
Parents are encouraged to have four named visitors as a support network who can visit until 21:00; these can be either grandparents or other important people in your lives. With your consent the named visitors can visit unaccompanied.

Family and friends...
Other visitors are welcome between 16:00-19:00 provided a parent is present.

Restrictions...
Baby’s brothers and sisters are welcome but to protect against infection no other children under the age of 16 are allowed to visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
On entering the unit please remove outdoor coats (place in the lockers provided), and wash your hands at the sink in the entrance area. As a further precaution use the alcohol gel available at the cot side before touching your baby.

We are keen to minimise the risk of babies catching infections from visitors, especially children, please speak to a member of the team if you or a visitor is unwell

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
We ask that you respect other visitors, noise levels, privacy and trust policy by not taking calls in clinical areas. Mobile phones can be used in the waiting room area but should not be used anywhere else on the unit (including the corridor or the unit reception area). Mobile phones can be used for taking photographs providing they are in flight mode.

Parent facilities...
There is a parent’s kitchen on the unit with free tea and coffee in addition to fridge and microwave facilities. Opposite the kitchen there is a sitting room with a soft play area for siblings. Leaflets and information are freely available in the reception area.

Parent accommodation...
There are two en-suite bedrooms on the unit these are used to allow parents to room in prior to babies being discharged. We have a further two rooms close to the unit, these rooms are generally used for parents who are a long way from home and need to be near their baby.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
The maternity unit is Baby Friendly UNICEF accredited and all of our staff are trained to provide support for mothers who are expressing or establishing breast feeding. We have a dedicated support worker whose role is to help mothers achieve a good milk supply for their baby. Breast pumps are loaned to mothers while their babies are on the unit, and milk can either be expressed at the cot side (with privacy screens) or in a dedicated private room. There is a fridge/freezer on the unit for the storage of expressed breast milk.

Facilities for siblings...
We have a family sitting room with a soft play area; however children must be supervised at all times.

Parent support group...
We have an active Bliss support group on the unit and Bliss Champions visit the unit twice weekly (Tuesday and Thursday).

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a team of chaplains trained in religious, spiritual and pastoral care who visit the unit on a regular basis. They can be contacted on 01274 365819 during office hours or alternatively speak to the nurse caring for your baby. Families are very welcome to use their own faith leader for support and prayers. There is a Chapel located in Field House and a Prayer Room in the main hospital at Bradford Royal Infirmary.

Play specialists...
We have access to play specialists via the paediatric wards if required.

Telephone and computer access...
There is a payphone available in the main reception of the maternity building. The unit is fitted with free WIFI in the parent areas.

Bereavement support...
There is an active bereavement support group within the unit who help to support parents during and after a loss. We help parents in creating memories and work closely with the hospices and chaplaincy to provide faith specific support to families. For further details please contact Susan Minchella (Senior Sister 01274 364522 susan.minchella@bthft.nhs.uk.)

Counselling...
We have two counselling suites on the unit available for private and personal discussions with the team caring for your baby. Please speak to a member of the team if you need to use these areas at other times.

Other important stuff...
We have a team of seven consultants; your baby will be admitted under the care of a named consultant but daily care will be managed by the consultant of the week. Your baby will have a nurse allocated to care for them each day.

What to expect

Ward round...
Daily ward rounds start at 09:00 for intensive care and high dependency babies. Babies in the low dependency areas have routine senior medical review at least twice weekly. Parents are welcome to join the discussion about their baby but to maintain confidentiality you will be asked to leave the unit when other babies are being discussed.

Baby rest time...
We have a quiet time on the unit between 13:00-15:00 in the afternoon to allow the babies to rest, during this time the lights are dimmed and the noise is reduced.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please feel free to ask to the nurse caring for your baby questions at any time. If you would like to speak with someone more senior ask to speak to the coordinator.

Talking to the medical team...
We aim to speak to parents soon after their baby is admitted and are available on ward rounds and at other times to discuss your baby’s care with you. There is a senior doctor available on the unit at all times of the day or night – if they appear busy or if you would like to speak to the consultant, please ask the nurse caring for your baby.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please ask to speak to the senior nurse on duty or arrange to meet with Matron (Sarah Szpara)

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
PALS offers free and confidential advice to parents, relatives and carers, the office is located on Level 1 in the main hospital at main reception. Telephone 01274 364021 or email pals@bthft.nhs.uk.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is a parent’s kitchen on the unit with free tea and coffee available. The kitchen also has fridge and microwave facilities, you are welcome to bring in and store your own food and drinks. Within the maternity unit there is a small café selling sandwiches and drinks Monday to Friday 09:00-16:00. In the main hospital the Hub café is located at the main reception and opens 07:30-19:30 (Monday to Friday) and 07:30-19:00 (Saturday & Sunday).

Other shops on site/nearby...
There are no shops on site at present but there are a number of shops and takeaways in the surrounding area including two large supermarkets. Please ask a member of staff for more information.

Cashpoint machine...
The cashpoint machine is located near the café and the public toilets on the ground floor of the Women’s & Newborn building.

Access to a midwife...
Face-to-face interpreting services are available 08:30-17:00 (Monday- Thursday) and 08:30-16:30 (Friday). Please speak with the nurse caring for your baby for out of hours services.

Interpreting services...
A telephone interpreting service is available for most languages and a face-to-face interpreter can be arranged via ward staff.

Laundry facilities...
For parents resident in the hotel rooms on M2, there are laundry facilities available in the adjacent kitchen.
