Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
Access to the unit is through the Women and Children's Department and take either lift five or the stairs to floor two then follow the directions for the neonatal unit.

Car parking charges...
There is a car park on Dryclough Lane off the main Huddersfield Road (HX3 0PW) alternatively there is parking at the entrance to Women and Children’s Department or A & E. In addition, there is parking on the roadside which is pay and display. Please note you cannot access the second floor by the main hospital entrance. If entering the building from this direction please ask at reception for directions to Women and Children’s Services, lift 5.

  • First 30 minutes are free
  • £2.50 for 2 hours
  • £3.70 for 4 hours

If your baby is a patient on the neonatal unit please speak to the nurse in charge for a parking permit which entitles you to free parking. The parking permit will need to be revalidated every four days.

Nearest bus stop...
The nearest bus stop is on the main Huddersfield Road outside the hospital and is served by buses from Halifax and Huddersfield (and vice versa). There is also a shuttle service between both hospital sites please ask for information on the ward for pick up points. This service is free however small children are not allowed to travel on this service.

Nearest railway station...
Halifax station is approximately three miles away and we suggest you take a taxi from the station to the hospital - the fare is approximately £ 5.00.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents have 24 hour access to the unit.

Number of visitors...
Strictly two visitors per cot.

Siblings...
Siblings are permitted for short periods between 11:00 and 19:00.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 11:00 and 18:00.

Family and friends...
Family and friends can visit between 18:00 and 19:00 however, visiting is restricted to no more than two people and this includes a parent.

Restrictions...
No children under the age of 16 years (except siblings) may visit the unit. In addition, any visitor who is unwell or has been in contact with any infectious diseases should speak to the nurse in charge before entering the unit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
The unit operates a strict policy of hand washing and the use of alcohol gel. We also ask that, before handling your baby, all watches, bracelets and dress rings should be removed. In addition, all outdoor clothing should be removed prior to entering the nursery.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones are not to be used in the clinical area for conversations and must be placed on silent mode.

Parent facilities...
We have a parent sitting room where tea and coffee is available free of charge and a separate quiet room (at the bottom end of the unit) which mothers can use to express breast milk.

Parent accommodation...
There are three parent rooms on the unit. These are used to provide overnight accommodation for parents of babies which are very sick or by parents who are preparing for discharge home.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
Breast pumps are available to lend, if required, for all mums on the neonatal unit to help them continue to express at home (bottles are provided free of charge). On site at Calderdale we have a breast milk bank which provides donor breast milk for sick and preterm babies. There is also a breast feeding café and experienced nurses on the unit to support mums with establishing feeding.

Facilities for siblings...
We have limited facilities for siblings.

Parent support group...
A Bliss volunteer visits the unit every week to support parents and there are information leaflets and contact numbers available on the ward. There is also a Baby Café which is a support group for breast feeding mums - further information is available on the ward or from Marilyn Rogers Infant Feeding Advisor 01422 224417.

Religious and spiritual support...
The hospital has a chapel and male/female prayer rooms located on first floor (use lift one - go through the double doors and the rooms are on your right). Spiritual advisors, Buddhists, Sikh, Muslim, Church of England and Roman Catholic chaplains are also available - please speak to your nurse.

Play specialists...
Unfortunately, there are no play specialists at Calderdale Hospital.

Telephone and computer access...
Ward direct line 01422 224421.

Bereavement support...
Forget-Me-Not Hospice provides pre and post bereavement support for parents, siblings, grandparents and their extended family. The hospice has facilities to help parents make memories with their child within the hospice or at home (Snowflake Care).

Counselling...
Bereavement counselling is provided by Forget-Me-Not Hospice.

Other important stuff...
Calderdale has a breast milk donor bank. If your baby should require donor breast milk this will be discussed with you.

What to expect

Ward round...
There is a ward round everyday between 09:00-11:00 and you are welcome to attend while your baby is being discussed.

Baby rest time...
The unit has a quiet time from 14:00 to 15:00 to allow babies the opportunity to rest and we ask that you respect this time.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
If you have any questions about your baby’s care, please speak to the nurse caring for your baby or the nurse-in-charge (who will be wearing a red badge).

Talking to the medical team...
The ward round is between 09:00-11:00 hours. If you cannot attend during these times a consultant is available on the unit during the day to answer any questions. Please ask the nurse in charge.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns, please speak to the nurse-in-charge or the Neonatal Unit Manager. Both these people will be happy to discuss any concerns you might have and answer any questions.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS service can be contacted on telephone 0800 0130018.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
The main restaurant (Ingleton Falls) is located on the ground floor and is open Monday-Friday 08.00-17:00 (10:00-14:00 on Saturday and Sunday). They also have a delivery service to the unit with a fresh daily menu. There is also a Costa Coffee shop near to the main entrance which is open Monday-Friday 07:30-20:00 (08:00-20:00 on Saturday and Sunday) and a League of Friends refreshment bar in the outpatients area which is open Monday- Friday 09:45-12:15 and 13:45-16:15 (closed at the weekend).

Other shops on site/nearby...
WH Smith (which stocks papers, magazines and snacks) is located on the ground floor. There is also a sandwich shop across the road from the hospitals main entrance car park who will deliver if needed.

Cashpoint machine
There is a cash machine located on the ground floor on the same side as WH Smith.

Access to a midwife...
A midwife service is available for postnatal ladies from the ward and those who have been discharged home. If you need to access this service, please speak to the nurse in charge of the unit.

Interpreting services...
Pearl Linguistics provide face-to-face and telephone translations. Written translation and Braille British sign language is provided by Big Word Services.

Laundry facilities...
Each baby has a laundry bag for their own clothes. Parents can either take their baby’s clothes home to be washed or leave them to be washed on the ward. However, the unit does not accept responsibility for clothes washed on the ward.
