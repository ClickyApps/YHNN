Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
Access is via the Scarsdale Wing entrance and then follow the signs to the Neonatal Unit which is located next to the birth centre.

Nearest car park...
There is lots of onsite parking available, the nearest car park for parents and visitors is car park 4.

Car parking charges...

  • 0-30 mins – free
  • 30 mins – 1 hour - £2.00
  • 1-2 hours - £2.70
  • 2-4 hours - £3.70
  • 4-24 hours - £5.70
  • 14 visit pass - £15.00
  • After 14 days on the Neonatal Unit a car parking card is issued giving 1 free visit per day – please speak to the nurse looking after your baby

Once a baby has been on the unit for 14 days a free parking pass (which has to be renewed every two weeks) can be obtained via nursing staff.

Nearest bus stop...
The nearest bus stop is located outside the hospital main entrance.

Nearest railway station...
The nearest train station is Chesterfield which is a 5 minute journey by car or taxi (approximately 30 minute walk).

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents and siblings are welcome to visit anytime.

Number of visitors...
Only three visitors per family are to be in the Neonatal Unit at any one time.

Siblings...
Siblings are welcome to visit anytime provided they are accompanied by a parent.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 15:00-21:00 unaccompanied by parents if permission has been given.

Family and friends...
Family and friends (over 16 years of age) can visit between 15:00-16:00 and 18:00-19:00. When parents are present on the unit.

Restrictions...
A maximum of three visitors per family to be on the unit at any one time and no visitors under the age of 16 (except siblings). Only parents are to hold and touch babies while they are in an incubator.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Hand washing and gel stations are available all around the unit. All visitors are asked to use the hand gel as they enter the unit and wash their hands using soap and water before touching the baby and after changing nappies. Visitors must not touch any baby other than their own. Any visitors who are unwell or have signs of infection must not visit. Please inform the Nurse in Charge and she will confirm whether or not it is safe for the infected person to visit.
Parents are asked to provide basic hygiene equipment for the baby (e.g Nappies, Nappy Sacks and when required Bepanthan Cream for nappy care) we do not recommend the use of baby wipes for nappy care

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
It is important that telephone calls are made off the unit in the public areas of the hospital as this helps to reduce noise levels which babies can find stressful. Mobile phones can be used to take photos of your baby.

Parent facilities...
The parents coffee room has a fridge and kettle. Parents can use the fridge to store their own food and free tea, coffee and juice is provided for all parents and siblings. When using the fridge, please label any food with your name and the date. Parents are expected to wash all crockery after use to ensure that clean supplies are available for the next parent. To prevent injury to the baby hot drinks must not be consumed at the cot side.

Parent accommodation...
We have two bedrooms available with shared bathroom facilities for parents to use prior to discharge. If your baby is very unwell you may also be asked if you wish to stay to be closer during a critical time. We also have residency accommodation if the parents live far away with attached bathroom and catering facilities.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
We have breast pumps available on the unit for expressing and these can be loaned (free of charge) to mums to use until their baby is discharged. It is important that breast pumps are returned promptly when the baby is discharged or when the pump is no longer needed.
If you choose to bottle feed your baby, the nursing staff will show you how to make up feeds safely. We recommend that you bring in the bottles your baby will use at home so baby can get use to these before they go home.

Facilities for siblings...
We have books and games, DVD’s please ask the nurse caring for your baby. There is also juice available for drinks in the parents sitting room.

Parent support group...
The parent support group meets five times per year on a Saturday morning, and have a variety of speakers at the meetings. Posters advertising these meetings are displayed in the parent sitting room. The unit has Bliss Parent Supporters who visit the unit they have personal experience of being the parent of a preterm baby who was admitted to the neonatal unit. Please ask the nurse caring for your baby for the dates and times the supporters will be on the unit.

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a Chapel in main concourse which is open 24 hours a day.

Play specialists...
The unit does not have play specialists but does have a team of nursery nurses who will work with parents to prepare them for discharge of their baby. They can advise on feeding, weaning, wrapped bathing, safe sleeping and car safety.

Telephone and computer access...
None.

Bereavement support...
We have access to Talking Therapies (Tel 0300 555 5582) and the chaplaincy team. Please ask for an information leaflet.

Counselling...
We have access to Talking Therapies on 0300 555 5582 - please ask for an information leaflet.

Other important information...
We only give information about a baby’s condition to the parents of that baby The hospital has nursing and medical students in training placements and a student under the supervision of a qualified nurse or doctor may care for your baby.
Some babies have more complex needs and, to ensure you are well supported after discharge it may be necessary to hold a discharge planning meeting with yourself and staff who will be caring for your baby in the community.
When your baby is ready for discharge you will be offered the opportunity to complete the friends and family questionnaire. This is an opportunity for you to feedback on our service, help us to improve and it is completely anonymous.
Babies born before 36 weeks gestation or with complex care needs will be supported at home following discharge by either the neonatal or the paediatric community team in conjunction with your health visitor.

Clinical lead...

Nurse manager...
Lynn Elliott, Matron, 01246 512515

Breastfeeding advisor...
All nursing staff are available to help and support breast feeding, if breast feeding support is needed after discharge staff can organise once permission has been given by yourself for a breast feeding supporter to visit you at home.

Family centred care/developmental care lead...
The unit provides holistic care specific to the needs of each baby and their family and every effort will be made to involve parents in the decisions about the care of your baby. The nursing staff will help you to co-ordinate your visiting with your baby’s feed and care times. The nursing staff will help parents to understand the changing development needs of their baby as they grown and they will help you to care for your baby in the way that encourages them to reach their milestones.
Caring for your baby in the neonatal unit a parents handbook is a unique book for all parents who have pre-term or newborn babies in need of hospital care, these books are available to purchase from the unit, please ask the nurse caring for your baby.

What to expect

Ward round...
We have a daily ward round from 09:00 onwards.

Baby rest time...
We encourage a quiet time from 14:00-16:00 and, to support your babies developmental care needs, we encourage minimal noise levels at all other times.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please speak to the nurse looking after your baby on the shift.

Talking to the medical team...
Your baby will be reviewed by the medical team as part of the daily ward round. During this time, you will have the opportunity to discuss your baby’s care and progress with the team. If this time is not convenient for you, please speak to the nurse in charge who can arrange a separate meeting for you to discuss your baby’s care.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please speak to either the nurse looking after your baby, the nurse in charge, the matron of the unit or any member of the medical team.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The assistance and complaints service is situated in the main entrance. Leaflets are also available on the ward.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is a Café@Royal in the main entrance and another one in the Scarsdale entrance. There is also a small café in the side entrance near to adult wards.

Other shops on site/nearby...
Shop@Royal selling newspapers and snacks is located in the main entrance along with a flower shop. Pharmacy is located on the yellow corridor in the main hospital where you are able to buy medicines, breast pumps and they also dispense GP prescriptions.

Cashpoint machine
The cashpoint machine is situated in the main hospital entrance.

Access to a midwife...
If mums require access to a midwife while visiting NNU please ask the nurse caring for your baby to facilitate this.

Interpreting services...
Face to face interpreter service is available via extension 3735, out of hours service 0800 7317878. If families require this service please contact the nurse in charge.

Laundry facilities...
The unit’s baby clothes and blankets are washed on the unit and hospital sheets and towels sent away to the laundry. Baby’s own clothes are placed in a bag attached to the cot for parents to take home to wash.
