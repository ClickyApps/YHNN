Halifax Road, Dewsbury, WF13 4HS
Hospital Switchboard: 0844 8118110
Unit Telephone Number: 01924 816045

How to access the unit...

The neonatal unit is on level 1 of the Bronte Tower. Please press the buzzer to gain entry.

Nearest Car Park...

In the hospital grounds there are a number of car parks (clearly signposted ) for patients to use.

Car Parking charges...

Parking charges are:

  • 1 hour - £2.00
  • 1 - 2 hours - £2.80
  • 2 - 4 hours - £5.00
  • 4 - 24 hours - £6.90

There is a reduced rate for parents of babies on the unit - £6.90 per week or £2 per day.

To obtain the reduced parking, please ask your allocated nurse for the relevant form.

Nearest Bus Stop...

The nearest bus stop is opposite the main entrance (bus number 268).

Nearest railway station...

Just on the outskirts of the town centre is Dewsbury Railway Station and it is recommended that you travel to the hospital by bus or taxi.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Unlimited access for parents.

Number of visitors...
Visiting is restricted to parents and two other visitors per cot.

Siblings...
Siblings can visit between 08:00–19:00 but must be accompanied by a parent.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 08:00–19:00.

Family and friends...
Friends can visit between the hours of 14:00-16:00 and 18:30-20:00 (please see the section on ‘number of visitors’).

Restrictions...
Children must be accompanied by a parent – other visitors 14.00pm – 19.00pm. children under the age of 14 (excluding siblings) are not allowed to visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Please make sure you are ‘bare below the elbow’ (coats and jumpers must be removed) and you adhere to general hygiene regulations ie sanitising hand rub.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Please make sure your phone is either switched off or on silent (flight) mode.

Parent facilities...
There is a kitchen area for parents to use which includes a fridge and a microwave. Tea and coffee are also available.

Parent accommodation...
There are two bedrooms for parents to use – please see nurse in charge of your baby.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
There is a breast feeding room on the neonatal unit and we also have an Infant Feeding Co-ordinator.

Facilities for siblings...
There is a play room on the unit which contains books, toys and games.

Parent support group...
It is hoped that a Bliss representative will soon be available on the unit.

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a hospital Chaplain and, if you would like to speak to him, please ask the nurse looking after your baby and she will arrange it. There is also a the Faith Centre which can be found on the first floor of the Ridings Building.

Play specialists...
There are no play specialists on the neonatal unit at Dewsbury Hospital.

Telephone and computer access...
There are no telephones for parents to use on the neonatal unit but we have WIFI – please refer to Nurse in charge for user name and password.

Bereavement support...
Bereavement support is offered by a specialist midwife. Please speak to the nurse in charge of your baby. We also have close links with the Forget me Not Hospice.

Counselling...
You will be directed to your midwife for counselling support and she will give help with this.

Other important stuff...
The neonatal outreach team are available seven days per week to provide support for families on discharge.

If you have any other questions – please feel free to ask your allocated nurse

Who’s who

Clinical lead...
Dr David Gibson, Lead Neonatologist. Telephone 01924 54192.

Nurse manager...
Louise Smith, Ward Manager. Telephone 01924 816045.

Breastfeeding advisor...
All nursing staff on the ward are trained in breastfeeding.

Family centred care/developmental care lead...
Please speak to the nurse allocated to your baby.

What to expect

Ward round...
Ward rounds take place each morning at around 09:00.

Baby rest time...
We do not have a fixed rest time for babies as this will be decided on an individual basis.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please speak to the nurse allocated to your baby.

Talking to the medical team...
There are ward rounds every day at around 09:00. Please feel free to ask to see the medical team if you have any issues or questions.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please speak to the nurse allocated to your baby, sister-in-charge or the unit manager.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS office is based at the Pinderfields Hospital on Level C – Telephone No. 01924 542972.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
Marks & Spencer café is located near the main entrance of the hospital and is open 08:30-20:00 (Monday-Friday) and 10:30-20:00 (Saturday and Sunday). There is also a WRVS café in the main entrance to the family services building which is open 08:30-18:00 (open Monday-Friday).

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a hospital run shop in the main entrance to hospital which sells gifts and cards, and a shop selling snacks and drinks in the entrance to the maternity unit.

Cashpoint machine
There is a cashpoint machine half way down the ground floor corridor of the main hospital.

Access to a midwife...
Access to a midwife is via the Delivery Suite or the post-natal ward – please refer to your baby’s allocated nurse for more information.

Interpreting services...
Face-to-face and telephone interpreting services are available. Please speak to the Nurse in Charge if this service is needed.

Laundry facilities...
Laundry facilities on the ward are for babies only. However, baby’s own clothes should be taken home to be washed.
