Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
After entering the main entrance of the hospital, take the 1st left then 1st right follow the signs for Family Services Building (women’s and children’s) and take the lift to the second floor. Come through the double doors and turn right to The Neonatal Unit. You will than need to press the intercom for access this is connected to a surveillance system. (Data is not recorded)

Nearest car park...
The nearest visitors car park is next to the Family Services building.

Car parking charges...
Car parking is available at £4.50 per day. Resident parents and parents of babies receiving intensive care can apply for a permit which will allow them to park free of charge. Other parents are given free parking after their baby has spent a week in hospital. Please ask a member of staff to complete the relevant form which should then be taken to the security office near the main entrance

Nearest bus stop...
The nearest bus stop is at the front of the hospital, near the main entrance.

Nearest railway station...
The nearest railway station is either Grimsby town centre or Cleethorpes which are both a bus or taxi ride from that hospital.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents have open visiting - 24 hours day.

Number of visitors...
Visiting is limited to two people per cot for intensive care patients and three people per cot in other nurseries (this includes parents).

Siblings...
Siblings can visit at any time provided they are accompanied by an adult

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between the hours of 14:00-16:00 and 18:30-20:00.

Family and friends...
Friends can visit between the hours of 14:00-16:00 and 18:30-20:00 (please see the section on ‘number of visitors’).

Restrictions...
Visitors are only allowed if the unit has gained permission from the parents of the baby and can be identified in their absence. Children (other than siblings) must be over the age of twelve

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Alcohol gel is available at several points throughout the unit and sinks available in every room so visitors can wash their hands before they touch baby. Anyone who has suffered from diarrhoea, vomiting, coughs or colds are advised not to visit for 72 hours or until symptoms disappear.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones can be used to take photographs or videos but the flash must be turned off and the phone must be on silent when in the nurseries. All calls must be taken off the unit

Parent facilities...
There is a kitchen which is equipped with a microwave, fridge, toaster and sandwich toaster and tea, coffee and cereals are provided for all resident parents free of charge. Parents can bring food from home, which should be labelled and stored in the fridge. There is also a water cooler outside the family room. The tea and coffee facilities are not for visitors

Parent accommodation...
We have 3 flats/rooms on the unit which are available for parents to use and one flat outside the unit which is shared with Rainforest (Children’s Ward). These are primarily reserved for parents of babies receiving intensive care, poorly babies and babies nursed away from their own geographic area. They can also be used for rooming in prior to baby being discharged home

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
There is a breastfeeding room on the neonatal unit with mobile expressing units. There is also a breastfeeding co-ordinator - Amanda Anderson (01472 875251 or mobile 07659510555) - who has a number of full or part-time assistants.

Facilities for siblings...
There is a family room located on the unit which has a TV/DVD player and a small number of toys and games.

Parent support group...
There is a parent information board outside the family room and leaflets in both the milk kitchen and the breastfeeding rooms. It is hoped that there will soon be Bliss representation on the neonatal unit.

Religious and spiritual support...
The chaplain visits the unit every Wednesday and parents are free to contact this service 24 hours a day for prayer and reflection (telephone 01472 875204).

Play specialists...
We do not have any play specialists at Grimsby Hospital.

Telephone and computer access...
TV, telephone and WiFi can be accessed via patientline.

Bereavement support...
We have close links with St Andrews Hospice ( for general enquiries telephone 01472 350908 )

Counselling...
Parents can be referred to their Health visitor, the Stillbirth and Neonatal Death Society (SANDS) or they can receive support from the midwifery team.

Other important stuff...
The unit can be accessed from another route off Second Avenue at the back of the hospital and there is also a gate at the rear of the hospital opening onto Scartho Road. Both these entrances are open 06:00-22:00 Monday-Friday but closed on bank holidays.

What to expect

Ward round...
A ward round takes place every day at 09:00 and parents are welcome to attend as this is an opportunity for the consultants to discuss any care or follow up baby may require.

Baby rest time...
We have a quiet hour between 13:00-14:00 every day where no nursing or clinical interventions take place (unless essential). Parents are encouraged to cuddle and have 1:1 time with their baby during this hour. We ask that parents arrive 10 minutes prior to quiet hour. If you do not wish it participate in quiet hour please respect those parents that do

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
You are welcome to talk to any member of the neonatal team about your baby’s care – consultants, doctors or nurses and are encouraged to attend the ward rounds which start at approximately 09.00.

Talking to the medical team...
You are welcome to speak to the consultant caring for your baby at any time – please speak to one of the nurses and they will arrange this on your behalf.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
You are welcome to talk to any member of the neonatal team.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PAL service is located in the reception area of the main hospital or can be contacted on 01472 875403.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
Costa Coffee is located near the main entrance of the hospital and is open 08:30-20:00 (Monday-Friday) and 10:30-20:00 (Saturday and Sunday). There is also a WRVS café in the main entrance to the family services building which is open 08:30-18:00 (open Monday-Friday)

Other shops on site/nearby...
A bakery, Co-op, post office and take away restaurants are nearby on Second Avenue which is a five minute walk from the family service building.

Cashpoint machine
The cashpoint machine is located in the main hospital, entrance opposite the family services building straight onwards near to the main staircase.

Access to a midwife...
Access to a midwife can be obtained via the maternity wards. If you need to access this service, please speak to a member of the nursing staff.

Interpreting services...
Interpreting services are available via telephone (the big word). If this method is not appropriate we can book an interpreter via PALS for a face to face meeting

Laundry facilities...
We currently have no laundry facilities onsite and the nearest laundrette a ten minute drive away on Ladysmith Road which also has free parking (telephone 01472 250758).
