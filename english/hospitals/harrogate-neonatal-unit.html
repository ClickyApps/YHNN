Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
The neonatal unit is located on the second floor of the hospital in the Strayside Wing. Follow the sign for Strayside Wing then, on the first floor when you come out of the lift or staircase, turn right and follow signs for SCBU.

Nearest car park...
There is a multi storey car park on site.

Car parking charges...

  • First 30 minutes free
  • 30 minutes to 1½ hours £2:40
  • £3.60 for 3½ hours and the charges increase by approximately £1:50 per hour

However we give parents (only) 50% off for the first week of a baby’s stay then free from two weeks. Parents can obtain a voucher from the unit which they need to present at the front desk by the main entrance to the hospital.

Nearest bus stop...
The nearest bus stop is on Knaresborough Road which is adjacent to the hospital

Nearest railway station...
We are equal distance from Starbeck and Harrogate station.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents have 24 hour open visiting.

Number of visitors...
Only two visitors at the cot side at any one time – this includes parents.

Siblings...
Siblings can visit between 16:00-17:00 and again from 18:00-19:00.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 16:00-17:00 and again from 18:00-19:00 .

Family and friends...
Aunts and uncles can visit between 12:00-18:00 but we regret that no other visitors are allowed.

Restrictions...
No other visitors are allowed except those mentioned below. Anyone who is feeling unwell, has a respiratory infection, diarrhoea or upset stomach must refrain from visiting and inform the nurse in charge.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
There are clear visible notices on the door to the unit and on the walls on the nurseries, and all visitors are required to wash their hands after entering the unit.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Use of mobile phones is allowed on the unit.

Parent facilities...
There is a shower room which can be used by parents staying over and occasionally by parents who have children on the children’s ward. There is also a kitchen which is available for use by all parents with babies on the neonatal unit plus parents with children on the children’s ward.

Parent accommodation...
We have two newly refurbished parent bedrooms, which can be used by parents whenever available.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
A breast feeding room is available for mum’s to use and nurses from the unit are trained to provide additional support.

Facilities for siblings...
There is a playroom on the children’s ward with a secure outside play area. There are a variety of toys and books that siblings are welcome to use as long as they are accompanied by a parent or grandparent.

Parent support group...
Support for parents is provided by Joy, our Bliss volunteer. She visits once a week (although this is a flexible arrangement) and full details can be found on her poster which is displayed on the unit.

Religious and spiritual support...
There is an on call chaplain available at all times and all faiths are represented. Contact can be made via the nursing staff.

Play specialists...
There are no play specialist available within the Trust.

Telephone and computer access...
Telephone and computer access is not available for parents and visitors.

Bereavement support...
Contact numbers for support groups can be obtained from nursing staff and midwives.

Counselling...
There are no counselling services provided by the Trust.

Other important stuff...
Baby massage available by Staff Nurse Debbie Beaty on SCBU and parents are also taught how to tube feed their baby.

What to expect

Ward round...
Ward rounds take place each morning between 09:00-10:00.

Baby rest time...
Baby’s rest time is between 15:00-17:00.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
If you have any queries about your baby’s care please talk to the nurse looking after your baby or the nurse in charge.

Talking to the medical team...
You can talk to a member of the medical team at any time, just ask the nurse caring for your baby.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns, please speak to neonatal unit manager or nurse in charge that day.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
To contact the PALS service, ring the hospital on 01423 555499 and ask for PALS on the automated call system.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
Herriot’s restaurant is the hospital canteen which is located on the first floor and there are clear signs showing the way. There is also a shop and a WRVS cafe at the main entrance which sells snacks and hot drinks together with various drinks machines scattered around the hospital.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a supermarket and takeaway outlets on Knaresborough Road which is near to the hospital. Please speak to a member of staff on the unit and they will give you directions.

Cashpoint machine
There is a cashpoint machine in the main foyer of the hospital.

Access to a midwife...
Access to a midwife on the postnatal ward is available if needed. Please speak to a nurse on the unit if you need to see a midwife.

Interpreting services...
Telephone interpreting services are available and these can be accessed via the nursing staff.

Laundry facilities...
Unfortunately there are no laundry facilities available to parents.
