How to access the unit...

The neonatal unit is on the second floor of The Women’s & Children’s Hospital and access is via the intercom at the main entrance.

Nearest Car Park...

Argyle Street hospital car park (HU3 2JZ) is the largest, and there is a small amount of visitor parking in front of hospital. There is also a medium sized pay & display car park on Fountain Street (HU3 2JZ) which is five minutes from the entrance (to the left of the Women’s and Children’s Hospital).

Car Parking charges...

  • Up to 1 hour - £1.50
  • Up to 2 hours - £2.50
  • 2-6 hours - £5.50 (this fee actually covers all day)

£10 / month for a display ticket for hospital car park – please ensure the ticket is displayed in the windscreen. Unfortunately, free parking has now stopped at Hull Royal Infirmary.

Nearest Bus Stop...

The nearest bus stop is directly outside the hospital on Anlaby Road, with buses going in both directions (city centre or out of Hull) All buses going into city centre go into the Interchange which is by the train station.

Nearest railway station...

The nearest railway station is in Hull centre which is a ten minute walk from the hospital next to the bus interchange. Alternatively, all buses passing the hospital will take you to the Interchange.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents can visit 24 hours a day. However, we do discourage routine visiting after 22:00 for baby’s rest and security reasons.

Number of visitors...
Due to infection control reasons and the lack of physical space, visiting is restricted to two people at the cot-side at any time.

Siblings...
Siblings can visit between 14:00-17:00 and again from 18:00-19:00.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 14:00-17:00 and again from 18:00-19:00.

Family and friends...
Unfortunately other family members and friends are not allowed to visit.

Restrictions...
Visiting is restricted to parents, grandparents and siblings and a maximum of two people per cot at any one time.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Visitors are encouraged wash their hands prior to entering the unit, wash them again before entering a clinical room and to use hand sanitiser on leaving a clinical room.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones can be used in the clinical rooms for everything except taking calls and we ask that these are taken in the waiting areas.

Parent facilities...
There is a small sitting room on the neonatal unit with facilities to make hot drinks, toast and snacks. Tea, coffee, milk and bread are provided free of charge and parents can bring their own meals to heat in the microwave.

Parent accommodation...
There are two rooming-in rooms with shared shower facilities. One double room with en-suite bathroom and one disabled double room with en-suite bathroom.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
There are portable breast pumps which can be loaned free of charge and hospital grade breast pumps are in every clinical room. There is also a dedicated expressing room with space for two mums. Catering freezers are available to store expressed milk and every mum will be allocated their own compartment.

Facilities for siblings...
We have a small waiting area with toys, books and a TV together with baby changing facilities. We also have a siblings diary system called ‘say hello’ which will be offered to you by the nurses caring for your baby.

Parent support group...
The Hull Bliss support group hold meetings on a monthly basis. There is also a poster on the Bliss display board at the entrance to the unit which is updated regularly by the Bliss volunteers who are available on the unit for support and information every Thursday and Friday evening/Saturday day.

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a chaplaincy service which covers the whole hospital and a small chapel on the ground floor of the main hospital (signposted from the main reception).

Play specialists...
Play specialists can be accessed from the paediatric wards. Please speak to the nurse looking after your baby if required.

Telephone and computer access...
Telephones on the neonatal unit can only be used for emergency calls or for telephone translation. There is no computer access for visitors and currently no wifi.

Bereavement support...
Support for bereaved families is provided by the neonatal outreach or the midwifery team. There is also the Crisis Pregnancy Centre, Newland Avenue, Hull HU52NB (01482 492929).

Counselling...
Bereavement counselling is provided by external agencies and no formal counselling is available via the hospital.

Other important stuff...
Family and friends questionnaires are given out prior to discharge and the monthly results are displayed on the unit.

What to expect

Ward round...
A ward round commences at 09:00 every day (starting in the ICU room) with a second handover round starting at 17:00.

Baby rest time...
Baby rest time take place between 14:00 and 16:00 each day.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
The nurse allocated to your baby on each shift.

Talking to the medical team...
You can talk to a member of the medical team at the ward rounds or by asking the nurse looking after your baby (or any member of the team) to arrange this on your behalf.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns, please speak to neonatal unit manager or nurse in charge that day.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS service is located at the Castle Hill site and contact details can be found on leaflets displayed in the waiting area on the Unit or by asking the nurse looking after your baby.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is a small café which on the ground floor of the Women’s and Children’s Hospital (open 09:00-16:30 Monday to Friday) and a small café on the ground floor of the main tower block (open 07:30-08:00 seven days a week), both of which serve a selection of sandwiches, salads, cakes, paninis and jacket potatoes. There is also a restaurant on the 2nd floor of the tower block which serves hot meals, sandwiches and snacks and is open 07:30-16:00 seven days a week.

Other shops on site/nearby...
WH Smiths directly in front of tower block sells drinks, snacks, magazines, stationary and a small selection of emergency toiletries are available. Alternatively, there is a small Boots chemist on the ground floor of the main hospital.

Cashpoint machine
There is a cash machine at the front of the main tower block.

Access to a midwife...
Access to a midwife can be arranged via the community office or maternity wards. Please ask the nurse looking after your baby if you need to arrange a midwife review.

Interpreting services...
A face to face or telephone interpreting service can be accessed via Global accent. Please speak the nurse looking after your baby if and when you require this service.

Laundry facilities...
Most laundry goes off site but baby clothes belonging to the unit are laundered on site. Baby’s own clothes are taken home by their parents and soiled items will be left in a patient clothing bag in the baby’s cupboard ready for collection.
