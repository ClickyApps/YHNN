Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
NICU is on level 2 of the Jessop Wing and is signposted from the lift area. Access is via the main doors and you will need to press the buzzer to gain access. Please bear with us as, at times, the staff are caring for a baby and cannot answer immediately.

Nearest car park...
There are limited parking spaces in the Jessop Wing pay and display car park (postcode for SatNav is S10 2SF) and the Royal Hallamshire Hospital (postcode for SatNav is S10 2JF) has a multi-storey car park (pay at exit). There is also some street parking on the side roads around the hospitals (parking meters).

Car parking charges...
All parents who have a baby on the neonatal unit are entitled to free parking in the Jessop Wing car park providing they display a permit in the windscreen of their car. Ask staff for a permit but please note, this permit does not guarantee a parking space. Other visitors will have to pay for parking in either the Jessop Wing (open 24 hours) or Hallamshire Hospital (open weekdays 06:30-22:30) so please bring plenty of change. Parking charges are currently £2.50 up to 2 hours, £3.70 up to 4 hours and £8.40 over 4 hours. Meter parking on roads around the Jessop Wing is available for up to 4 hours

Nearest bus stop...
The Jessop Wing is served by the 51, 52 and 95 buses which stop outside Weston Park Hospital (five minute walk) and the Hallamshire Hospital is served by the 60, 120 and 10 buses which stop close to the main hospital building.

Nearest railway station...
Sheffield station is two miles from the Jessop Wing and your journey can be completed by either bus or supertram.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents are encouraged to visit at any time.

Number of visitors...
We ask that a maximum of two people are by the cot side at any one time because space is limited in the unit. Other visitors can use the neonatal waiting rooms and ‘take turns’ to visit your baby.

Siblings...
Brothers and sisters of your baby can visit at any time, provided they are accompanied by a relative.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit at any time.

Family and friends...
If you would like any of your family and friends to visit without you being present, please tell the nurse caring for your baby and a record will be made of this request. Without this record, your visitors will only be able to visit if you are present.

Restrictions...
While we welcome visitors, we do ask that children under the age of 14 years who are not brother or sister of the baby do not come to the unit due to the risk of infection. Please also see the section below on ‘Hygiene and Infection Prevention’.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
All visitors to the neonatal unit are asked to help us reduce the risk of infection by using the alcohol hand rub when entering and leaving the neonatal unit and also when entering the nursery your baby is in.
On arrival we ask that coats are hung on the pegs inside the neonatal unit, and bags/valuables stored in the secure lockers at the entrance to the unit.
Before touching your baby we ask that you wash your hands and ensure all jewellery from hands and wrists is removed. This can also be stored in the lockers.
If you or one of your visitors are unwell with tummy upset, cold sores, sore throat, coughs, colds or have been in contact with chicken pox, please call the unit to see if it is safe for them or you (if you have had contact) to visit.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
You are able to use your phone to take pictures on the neonatal unit. However, please respect other families by using the waiting rooms (not the nurseries) to take or receive telephone calls.

Parent facilities...
There is a vending machine which has hot and cold drinks in the family room (located inside the ward area).

Parent accommodation...
We currently have six parents’ rooms (located on a corridor just outside of the main unit) which all have a toilet and washbasin. Separate shower rooms are located on the parent corridor, as is a parent kitchen which has a microwave, kettle, fridge and freezer. Cereals, milk, tea and coffee are provided for breakfast. Very occasionally we are unable to provide onsite accommodation for parents wishing to be resident. On these occasions we may be able to offer accommodation near the Hallamshire Hospital for a minimal charge. If this charge proves difficult for you The Chaplaincy Charitable Trust, The Sunday Fund, may be able to assist you.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...

Facilities for siblings...
We have toys for siblings to play with in both the waiting area (outside the unit) and in the family room (inside the ward). We also have sibling packs – please speak to one of the nurses if you would like one.

Parent support group...
The Jessop Wing has a support group – the Sheffield Little Miracles group - run via the Bliss charity, which provides support for local parents who have a baby on the unit and would like to meet other parents
The Sheffield Little Miracles group is run by parents of ex premature babies. Grandparents, siblings and friends are all welcome. The Little Miracles network has three sections: ‘Little Miracles’ (main community page), ‘Little Miracles Parent to Parent’ and ‘O2 Kids’ for families with children on home oxygen or home ventilation.
A Bliss volunteer also visits the neonatal unit weekly to provide support- please ask for details.

For more information contact: sheffieldlittlemiracles@yahoo.co.uk or visit website http://www.bliss.org.uk/sheffield-bliss-family-group or Facebook (Little Miracles Sheffield Bliss Support Group)
Bliss charity helpline 0500 618140 www.bliss.org.uk

Religious and spiritual support...
The chaplaincy services consist of a team of NHS staff who can provide spiritual care and religious support for all families on the neonatal unit. The team includes chaplains from the main traditions including Christian, Muslim and Buddhist faiths and support is available 24 hours a day. Chaplains are available to all families; you do not have to be religious to ask for support and there is a chapel (which contains multi-faith prayer rooms) located on A floor of the Royal Hallamshire where services are held at 11:00 every Sunday. There are also Muslim prayer rooms on B floor corridor (to the left of the shops).

Play specialists...
We do not have any play specialists but toys are available in both waiting rooms.

Counselling...
Families are offered an appointment to see one of the neonatal consultants approximately six weeks after their bereavement for support and follow up.

Other important information...
Financial support – staff on the unit will help you apply for a grant from the Family Fund which is the largest provider of grants to families who have seriously ill children. Families may be able to claim travel costs if they receive certain benefits or if they are bringing breast milk to their baby. If you feel this may apply to you then please see the nurse in charge of the unit or The Bliss Sheffield Hospital Charity Sister.
The Bliss Sheffield Hospital Charity Sister (Sandra Brown) is available to provide practical advice, emotional support and guidance to the families of the babies on the unit. This includes one to one support with expressing, breast feeding, skin-to-skin and parent craft. As well as supporting families Sandra works closely with the nurses and doctors and other clinical staff to promote family centered care on the unit. Sandra can be contacted on 0114 2268759 or at sandra.brown@sth.nhs.uk

What to expect

Ward round...
Ward rounds take place throughout the day and you are encouraged to stay in the nurseries while the ward rounds are in progress.

Baby rest time...
When your baby is sleeping, covers are placed over the cot or incubator to reduce noise and protect your baby from bright lights.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Either the doctor or the nurse looking after your baby will be able to talk to you about your baby’s care and any treatments that may be needed - please ask at any time.

Talking to the medical team...
You can speak to a doctor on a daily basis and you are welcome to participate in discussions about your baby during ward rounds. Please also feel free to ask for an appointment to discuss your baby’s care with a consultant or a senior doctor at any time.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns please speak to the nurse looking after your baby or the nurse in charge of the unit.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The team can be contacted Monday to Friday 09:00-17:00. Outside these times you can leave messages for the team to respond to on the next working day

  • Telephone on 0114 271 2400
  • Via email on pst@sth.nhs.uk
  • In person in the Patient Partnership Department on B floor, Royal Hallamshire Hospital

Cashpoint machine

Access to a midwife...
There is a maternity assessment unit on site which is open Monday to Friday 08:30-16:30 and staff can arrange an appointment if necessary.

Interpreting services...
A telephone interpreting service is available for most languages and a face-to-face interpreter can be arranged via ward staff.

Laundry facilities...
Baby clothes are washed on the Unit. There are no laundry facilities for parents.
