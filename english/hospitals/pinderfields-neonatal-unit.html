Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
The neonatal unit is on level C, Gate 19.You will need to press the buzzer to gain access –Please bear with us, as at times the staff are caring for a baby and cannot answer immediately.
All the staff are here to help you with any queries or problems you may encounter so please ask if you need help.

Nearest car park...
In the hospital grounds there are a number of car parks (clearly signposted ) for patients to use.

Car parking charges are...

  • 1 hour - £2.00
  • 1 - 2 hours - £2.80
  • 2 - 4 hours - £5.00
  • 4 - 24 hours - £6.90

There is a reduced rate for parents of babies on the unit - £6.90 per week or £2 per day (Dec 2016 data and may be subject to change)

To obtain the reduced parking, please ask your allocated nurse for the relevant form.

Nearest bus stop...
The nearest bus stop is in front of the hospital – go out through the main atrium revolving doors and turn left. Bus no 111 goes to the station and there is a free shuttle bus running between Pinderfields and Dewsbury hospitals

Nearest railway station...
There are two stations near Wakefield town centre – Wakefield Westgate and Wakefield Kirkgate – and it is recommended that you then travel to the hospital by bus or taxi

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents can visit at any time

Number of visitors...
Visiting is restricted to 4 people per cot side and must be accompanied by a parent

Siblings...
Siblings can visit between 08:00–19:00 but must be accompanied by a parent.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 08:00–19:00.

Family and friends...
Family and friends can visit between 14:00–19:00 provided a parent is present.

Restrictions...
Children must be accompanied by a parent – other visitors 14.00pm – 19.00pm. children under the age of 14 (excluding siblings) are not allowed to visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Please make sure you are ‘bare below the elbow’ (coats and jumpers must be removed) and you adhere to general hygiene regulations ie sanitising hand rub.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Please make sure your phone is either switched off or on silent airplane mode whilst on the Unit. They can still be used in camera mode and free Wi-Fi is available

Parent facilities...
There is a small kitchen area available for parents to use which includes a fridge and a microwave. Tea and coffee are also available but please do not bring in hot drinks/food into the nurseries. A cold water dispenser is at the entrance of the Neonatal Unit

Parent accommodation...
There are two bedrooms for parents to provide overnight accommodation when their baby is critically ill or getting ready for discharge

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
There are breast feeding rooms on the neonatal unit and screens are available if you would like to express by the cot side .We have an Infant Feeding Co-ordinator and support staff to help with feeding issues

Facilities for siblings...
There is a room which contains toys, books and games that can be used during the visit

Parent support group...
There is a Bliss Co-ordinator on the neonatal unit – please ask a member of nursing staff for details

Religious and spiritual support...
There is a hospital Chaplain (John Arnold. Contact telephone number 01924524567) and a place of worship on level D Gate 35.

Play specialists...
There are no play specialists on the neonatal unit at Pinderfields Hospital but if required can be accessed from the Paediatric Unit

Telephone and computer access...
There are no telephones for parents to use on the neonatal unit but we have WIFI – please refer to Nurse in charge for user name and password.

Bereavement support...
Bereavement support is offered by a specialist midwife. Please speak to the nurse in charge of your baby. We also have close links with the Forget Me Not Hospice and Martin House

Counselling...
You will be directed to your midwife for counselling support and she will give help with this.

Other important stuff...
The Neonatal Outreach Sisters have experience in neonatal care and provide a specialist service to you once your baby is discharged. They are available seven days per week. You will be introduced to the Neonatal Outreach Sister caring for you and your baby during your stay on the Neonatal Unit.

If you have any other questions – please feel free to ask your allocated nurse.

What to expect

Ward round...
Ward rounds take place each morning at around 09:00.

Baby rest time...
We do not have a fixed rest time for babies as this will be decided on an individual basis.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please speak to the nurse allocated to your baby.

Talking to the medical team...
There are ward rounds every day at around 09:00. Please feel free to ask to see the medical team if you have any issues or questions.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please speak to the nurse allocated to your baby, sister-in-charge or the unit manager.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS office is on Level C of Pinderfields Hospital– Telephone No. 01924 542972.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
There is an on-site restaurant on the ground floor of Pinderfields and vending machines throughout the hospital which sell drinks and snacks.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a WH Smith and Coffee shop on the ground floor in the hospital atrium near the lifts

Cashpoint machine
There is a cashpoint machine in the main hospital atrium near the external revolving doors.

Access to a midwife...
Access to a midwife is via the Delivery Suite or the post-natal ward – please refer to your baby’s allocated nurse for more information.

Interpreting services...
Face to face and telephone interpreting services are available. Please refer to Nurse in Charge.

Laundry facilities...
Laundry facilities on the ward are for babies only but this does not include their own clothes which should be taken home to be washed.
