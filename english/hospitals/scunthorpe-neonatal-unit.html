Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
Use the main Church Lane entrance and take the lift to E floor and the neonatal unit is the first ward on your right.

Nearest car park...
It is a secure unit with CCTV and an intercom system. All unknown visitors will not be allowed access.

Car parking charges...
There are three car parks – two on Church Lane side of the hospital (post code DN17 5BH) which are the best to use to access NICU. There is also a free Park and Ride service available.

  • Blue Badge £2.00 (fixed charge)
  • 1 hour - £2.00
  • 1 - 2 hours - £3.50
  • 2 - 4 hours - £4.00
  • 4 hours+ - £4.50 (Day ticket)
  • Weekly ticket - £22.50

Parents are given free parking after their baby has been in hospital for over a week. A form is obtained from NICU staff and should be taken to Security in A/E to obtain the pass.

Nearest bus stop...
The hospital shuttle stops outside the main entrance on Church Lane side of the hospital. This bus runs from the bus station and a set route around Scunthorpe.

Nearest railway station...
Scunthorpe train station is a 10 minute walk from the hospital.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents have open visiting and there is a quiet hour between 13:00-14:00 which is their special time for cuddles. There is no medical or nursing intervention during this time (unless an emergency) and the hour is used to promote bonding and skin-to-skin contact.

Number of visitors...
Visiting is restricted to three people per cot side. If more visitors arrive, we ask that they use the visitor sitting room and rotate to see baby.

Siblings...
Siblings of all ages can visit at any time, depending on their age and maturity they must be accompanied by an adult.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 14:00-16:00 and 18:30-20:00 with parents’ permission.

Family and friends...
Family and friends can visit between 14:00-16:00 and 18:30-20:00 with parents’ permission.

Restrictions...
Visitors are only allowed if we have parents’ permission. Parents can identify and give permission for visitors to attend in their absence. Children under the age of 12 (except siblings) are not allowed to visit.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Please do not visit the unit if you have any signs or symptoms of infection. Strict hand washing rules apply at all times.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Phones not are to be used on the unit, except to take photos. Any calls should be made or taken outside the unit.

Parent facilities...
Facilities for parents include - flats, open visiting, sitting rooms, dining room, parking, meals and breast pumps (loaned free of charge but must be returned prior to baby’s discharge). Also a nursing environment that promotes family-centred care.

Parent accommodation...
We have two flats available where both parents can stay and these have access to a shower, a toilet and a kitchen. In some circumstances siblings, birthing partners or those identified by mum as their support person can stay. The flats are allocated according to the following criteria - patient care level, rooming in prior to discharge, establishing breastfeeding or distance from home to the hospital. If none of these criteria are an issue (and the flats are available) any parent can stay at any time.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
We have a supply of breast pumps on the unit for mums to use together with the associated equipment (bottles, Milton tanks etc) and specialist breastfeeding chairs and cushions. All our breast pumps are electric and dual pumps and can be loaned for home use while baby is an in-patient. We also support and encourage skin-to-skin contact and expressing at the cot side (screens are available for privacy). We do however have a quiet room that can be used if mums prefer a private room. All nursing staff have undergone the Unicef breastfeeding course and are available to give support 24 hours a day. We also have access to ‘BABES’ (breastfeeding support while in hospital and post discharge), local breastfeeding groups and Cafes post discharge.

Facilities for siblings...
Facilities for siblings include – a playroom on the unit, sibling diaries, contact with the baby, photographs and the use of paper for colouring pictures etc.

Parent support group...
The neonatal unit at Scunthorpe will soon have its own Bliss volunteer and there is a Prem group which holds monthly meetings for families and the community neonatal team after discharge. We also access to ‘BABES which is a breastfeeding support group who can visit families while on the unit and post discharge.

Religious and spiritual support...
The hospital has a chapel and also a hospital chaplain who can be contacted via switchboard. All faiths are supported and we welcome all religions.

Play specialists...
Play specialist are available on the paediatric ward and will come to the neonatal unit if workload allows.

Telephone and computer access...
There is a public telephone on the ward but no routine computer access.

Bereavement support...
Bereavement support is provided by neonatal staff or the hospital chaplain. We hold an annual Remembrance Service at a local church and there is a Baby Book of Remembrance in the hospital. We also have access to a Specialised Health Visitor who has a bereavement care role.

Counselling...
We do not have any formal counsellors on the ward but staff are always available to support parents and direct them to national groups or the local specialist Health Visitor. The neonatal community team can also offer support post discharge.

What to expect

Ward round...
We have a daily ward round which is either Consultant or Registrar led, this occurs in the morning, usually from 10:00. Parents are welcome to be present while their baby is being discussed but for confidentiality reasons, they will be asked to leave the nursery while the medical staff review other babies.

Baby rest time...
A quiet hour with parents is between 13:00-14:00. Generally we encourage babies to rest for large periods of the day by grouping cares and restricting disturbance and clinical procedures.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Nursing staff are always available at all times. Medical staff are available according to their workload but will always try to attend if possible to speak to parents.

Talking to the medical team...
Parents can speak to medical staff during the daily ward round or, if the parents are not present during the ward round, nursing staff will contact the doctors and arrange for them to come back to the unit to see parents. If you would like to see the Consultant looking after your baby, please speak to a nurse and they will arrange a date and time which is convenient for everyone.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Depending on the circumstances we encourage parents to speak to the nurse caring for their baby that day or the shift lead. If more appropriate speak to Caroline Walker (Deputy Ward Manager) or Sarah Judd (Ward Manager).

We aim to solve any problems at source on the unit and in a timely manner. If parents feel they need to escalate problems higher we can direct them to Operational Matron, the Head of Children Services or PALS

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS service is located on floor C, outside outpatients.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
On the unit there is a parent’s kitchen with fridge, microwave and toaster and parents can make drinks anytime and utilise any of the three sitting rooms. If parents are resident (or staying all day), meals can be ordered and they are delivered to the unit. All this is free of charge.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There are small shops selling drinks, confectionary etc at the Church Lane entrance to the hospital. There is also a restaurant which is open Monday to Friday 08.30-14.00 (closed Saturday and Sunday) and the Coffee Bean coffee bar which is open Monday to Friday 08:30-19:00 (Saturday- Sunday 10:30-17:30). Both sell refreshments, meals and a selection of sandwiches and both are on D floor of the hospital.

Within a five minute walk from the hospital there is a local corner shop, a fish and chip shop and local Sainsbury store. Within a ten minute walk you will be in the town centre. Five minute drive will take you to a shopping centre with a large Tesco Extra and a variety of shops and restaurants

Cashpoint machine
There is a cashpoint machine by the main entrance (on the Church Lane side of the hospital) - near the lifts and shop.

Access to a midwife...
Midwives are available and postnatal checks can be completed (by arrangement) while visiting your baby.

Interpreting services...
We have the use of BIG WORD translator telephone service which is available 24 hours a day. If this method is not appropriate, we can book an interpreter for a face-to-face meeting.

Laundry facilities...
Laundry facilities are not routinely offered on-site.
