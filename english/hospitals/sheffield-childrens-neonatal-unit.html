Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
Sheffield Children’s Hospital is built on numerous levels and to access Neonatal Surgical Unit (NSU) is via Emergency Department (ED). Walk through the double doors and either walk down the stairs in front of you or take a lift down to B- level. Follow the corridor right round theatres then take a lift to C floor. On leaving the lift, turn right and buzz for NSU (we are part of Paediatric Critical Care).

Nearest car park...
The Children’s Hospital currently does not have a designated car park although one is under construction and, once built, there will have spaces for parents.

Nearest bus stop...
The 52 bus stops outside ED (Hillsborough terminal to Woodhouse via city centre) the other way.

Nearest railway station...
The nearest railway station is Sheffield Midland Station then take the tram to the Children’s Hospital (exit tram at the University stop).

Visiting Information

Parents...
We have open visiting for parents and primary carers.

Number of visitors...
Visiting is restricted to two people per bed space and only parents and primary carers are permitted to visit between the hours of 19:30-09:00.

Siblings...
Siblings may visit but remain the responsibility of parents or carers at all times.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit (with parent’s permission) between the hours of 08:30-19:30.

Family and friends...
Friends and family can visit between the hours of 08:30-19:30 provided a pre-arranged parent/care agreement has been documented.

Restrictions...
Please do not visit the ward if you have symptoms of cold, flu, diarrhoea or vomiting as our babies can be very susceptible to infections and virus’. Children under the age of 16 (who are not your baby’s siblings) are not allowed to visit for infection reasons. Other visitors may only visit with pre-approved consent from parents or primary carers

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Please wash and dry your hands when entering the ward and before touching your baby.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones must not to be used to make phone calls on the unit.

Parent facilities...
There is a waiting room which is shared with paediatric critical care. Enter the main door for PCCU, turn right and the waiting room is along the corridor.

Parent accommodation...
There are eleven rooms available in Magnolia House for parents who have children on critical care. Please speak to the nurse caring for your baby.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
Two of our senior sisters (Sue Payne and Bianca Bane) lead on breastfeeding and a hospital infant feeding advisor is also available (telephone 0114 3053137). There is also a breast feeding room on the ward.

Facilities for siblings...
There are toys available on NSU for siblings to play with and we share a waiting room with intensive care and high dependency which is located along the corridor which also has toys available.

Parent support group...
We will shortly be having a BLISS volunteer to help support parents and the hospital also has a PALS officer who can contacted on 01142717594.

Religious and spiritual support...
Religious and spiritual support is provided by our hospital chaplain (contact via switchboard 0114 2717000).

Play specialists...
Although we have no play therapists on the unit we can seek advice from our colleagues next door on Paediatric HDU. Please speak to the nurse in charge if you would like to access this service.

Telephone and computer access...
Unfortunately there is no telephone or computer access for parents on the ward. There is however a telephone available in main entrance.

Bereavement support...
Rose Cottage can offer bereavement support - just ask the nurse in charge.

Counselling...
If you need to talk to someone we have specialist s in the hospital who can come and listen to you and talk with you. Please ask the nursing staff if you would like to access this service.

Other important stuff...
Julie Olpin is our nurse specialist and she will help prepare you and your baby for home and offer support at follow up appointments.

What to expect

Ward round...
Surgical ward rounds take place each morning at around 08:30 except on Tuesdays when it is at 09:30. The ward rounds are also teaching sessions and for that reason, together with confidentiality, parents are not allowed to be present and the nurses and doctors will update you after the rounds. We also have a number of other teams of doctors who will come to review the babies at various times during the day.

Baby rest time...
We try to cluster baby’s care to allow babies long periods of rest which helps to conserve their calories for growing.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Talk to your baby’s nurse and ask to see a doctor or ask to speak to the ward manager.

Talking to the medical team...
Our doctors cover different areas in the hospital so we may have to arrange a time for them to come and speak to you and they can be contacted via the ward staff on 0114 2717324. Our neonatologists cover both NSU and the neonatal unit at the Jessop Wing.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
If you have any concerns please speak to the nurse caring for you baby, the nurse in charge or the ward manager.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS officer can contacted on 01142717594.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
The hospital Parkside restaurant is located near the outpatients department and is open between 08:00-11:30, 12:00-14:30 and 15:30-19:00. There is a small cafe in the main entrance which is open Monday – Friday between 09:00-15:30 and there is also a meal delivery service available – please ask the staff on the unit about it.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a supermarket near the University tram stop and shops in the University Student Union. Also there are a large selection of shops, takeaways etc in the area of Broomhill which is a 5-10minute walk from the hospital. Please ask the staff on the unit for directions.

Cashpoint machine
Cash point machines are located at the University Student Union, the Royal Hallamshire Hospital and Sainsbury’s (next to the tram stop). The Parkside restaurant in the hospital offers a free cashback service if you spend more than £5 on your debit card.

Access to a midwife...
We do not have midwives on site, however The Jessop Wing nearby has midwives on site who may see you. Please keep your hand held notes with you (contact via Jessop Wing Community Midwives 0114 2268301).

Interpreting services...
Language line is available – please speak to the nurse in charge if you would like to access this service.

Laundry facilities...
Baby’s bed linen is washed on the ward but baby’s clothes need to be laundered at home.
