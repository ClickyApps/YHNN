How to access the unit...

The neonatal unit is located on level 5 of the Gledhow Wing and this can be accessed via a lift (or by stairs) from the main reception area. There is a secure telephone camera entrance system and visitors are asked to ring the bell to gain admission

Nearest Car Park...

Parents are offered a parking permit for the duration of their stay in the neonatal unit, this is on site this is located in the multi story car park close to Bexley Wing, there are directions within the grounds. There are numerous other pay and display car parks around the hospital site and visitors can use the multi-story car park on site. The car parks off site are run by Leeds City Council and prices start at £2 and increase in hourly intervals. The on-site car park charges start at £2.50 to a maximum of £16 per day.

Car Parking charges...

Parking charges vary from £2.50 per day to £16 per day. The car park at the back of the hospital on Dolly Lane is the cheapest but this involves a significant walk.

Nearest Bus Stop...

The nearest bus stop is at the end to the drive leading to the Gledhow Wing. Buses go into Leeds city centre and out to the north and east of Leeds and the city centre.

Nearest railway station...

The nearest train station is in Leeds city centre which is a five minute bus ride (or a thirty minute walk) away.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents are allowed to visit their baby at any time.

Number of visitors...
We have to limit the number of visitors to two people per bed space but we do allow some flexibility under certain circumstances.

Siblings...
Siblings (baby’s brothers and sisters) are allowed to visit between the hours of 15:00-19:00. This is extended to 12:00–19:00 during school holidays.

Grandparents...
Baby’s grandparents are allowed to visit between the hours of 15:00-19:00.

Family and friends...
Other family members and friends are allowed to visit between the hours of 15:00-19:00 if accompanied by one of the parents.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
Families are taught clinical hand washing using soap and gel which should be practised on entering the unit and before and after each contact with their baby.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Parents and visitors are requested to take calls away from the clinical bays. If you are unsure, a member of staff will be able to advice you of where would be appropriate for you to take the call.

Parent facilities...
We have three rooms which can be used as bedrooms, a parent’s waiting areas and two kitchen areas which are located on the unit close to the entrance.

Parent accommodation...
We have three designated parent rooms. Two are multi-function rooms which offer communal and private spaces and can be used for private conversations, rooming in, breast feeding and expressing of milk. One of these rooms has en suite facilities but there is also a shower and a separate toilet on the ward for families and visitors to use.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
All staff on the neonatal unit are trained to provide support for breastfeeding and some have received additional training. We also have links with the midwives on the wards to provide additional support for mums when, in certain situations, early expression (before baby is born) is recommended i.e. diabetic mums are actively encouraged to start expressing from 36 weeks to produce a stock of milk for use immediately after birth. This reduces the need for intravenous fluids and formula milk.

Facilities for siblings...
We have a limited number of toys available for siblings to play with together with drawing packs and a small library of children’s books.

Parent support group...
Bliss support is available on the ward weekly these are ex parents who have had preterm infants they are a source of support and advice for families with babies currently on the unit. Families are encouraged to join forums, face book etc for an alternative source of support.

Religious and spiritual support...
All religions are supported in Leeds Teaching Hospital Trust. There are prayer rooms available and an onsite chapel in the grounds of St James close to the Gledhow Wing. There are also prayer facilities in the Bexley Wing of St James.

Play specialists...
There are no play specialists at St James Hospital.

Telephone and computer access...
We will soon have a tablet which can be used by parents to Skype or face time, BadgerNet family diary is also soon to be available for families.

Bereavement support...
We do not have any designated professional support at St James’s Hospitals but we do have a bereavement office on level 4 of the Gledhow Wing which provides advice and guidance. The neonatal unit also offers bereavement boxes and a variety of information leaflets.
All bereaved families are offered an appointment with a consultant neonatologist one month after their baby’s death and we can also arrange a meeting with the obstetric team if this is required. Further meetings can be arranged according to the family’s needs and counselling sessions (in respect of future pregnancies) can be arranged.

Counselling...
Counselling can be arranged according to individual needs.

Other important stuff...
Our neonatal unit is part of a regional service and we therefore have access to a full multidisciplinary team although some services are not on available on site. We have dedicated time from speech and language therapists, dietitians, occupation therapists and physiotherapists – all on a weekly basis.
St James Hospital is predominantly a high-to-low dependency unit with two short term ventilation cots. There are plans to centralise neonatal services in Leeds but no date for these changes is known at present.

Who’s who

Clinical lead...
Dr Sharon English, Clinical lead for Neonatal and Children’s Services (telephone 0113 2069101).

Nurse manager...
Jacky Riley, Nurse Manager (telephone 0113 2069101 Mon-Fri 08:00-16:00).

Breastfeeding advisor...
The breastfeeding lead for Leeds Teaching Hospital Trust is Susan Wallis and the breastfeeding facilitator of St James hospital is Kathy Dewhirst. All staff on the neonatal unit receive ongoing support, training and education in breastfeeding.

Family centred care/developmental care lead...
The family centred and developmental care lead is Dr Liz Mckechnie and Sister Maureen Hayton. We operate a family integrated care package which is structured around parent craft and education packages and shifts the responsibility for care of the baby over to the family. The aim is to build confidence, ease the transition from hospital to home, improve breast feeding and reduce re-admission to hospital in the first few weeks after birth.

What to expect

Ward round...
The time of ward rounds on the unit varies from day-to-day:

  • Monday - consultant led ward round for the whole unit starting in the office at 09:00 and moving onto the ward by 10:00
  • Tuesday – Consultant led ‘board’ round for intensive and high dependency patients starting at 14:00. This round includes doctors, nurses, dietitian, physiotherapists and outreach staff
  • Wednesday - Intensive and high dependency patients led by the consultant
  • Thursday – Doctor led ward round 09:00 for intensive and high dependency patients. Nurse led ward round 10:00 for low dependency patients
  • Friday - full ward round 09:00

Baby rest time...
Babies are encouraged to rest at all times and the ward is usually darkened and quiet to facilitate this.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Neonatal staff are more than happy to talk to parents about their baby’s care. Please ask one of the nurses and they will make arrangements for you to speak to the appropriate person.

Talking to the medical team...
The medical team are available during ward round (for times, please see separate section) or by appointment on an ad hoc basis.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
We are soon to have a BLISS champion who you can talk to if you have any concerns. We also have access to psychology services and representatives from various religious groups.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
Details of the Patient Advocacy Liaison Service provided by Leeds Teaching Hospitals Trust can be found in the parent’s rooms on the neonatal unit. The telephone numbers for PALs are 01132066261 /01132067168 and the system can also be accessed via the trust web site. Complaints telephone line is 0113 2066018 or can be placed in writing to the Chief Executive, Trust Headquarters, St James Hospital, Leeds.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
The neonatal unit provides free tea and coffee for parents in a dedicated parent facility and each day that their baby is on the unit, breast feeding mothers are issued with a meal voucher which can be exchanged at the canteen in the Gledhow Wing this is located on level 2 near the reception area. Other family members are also offered a discount voucher for this facility. The Gledhow Wing also has a Marks and Spencer food hall, a shop for snacks drinks and sandwiches and a Costa Coffee. Other wings have similar facilities and there is a Boots pharmacy in Chancellor Wing which stocks prescription and non-prescription medication and other products including snacks.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There are no off-site shopping facilities within close proximity to St James Hospital. However, Leeds city centre is a five minute bus ride (or a thirty minute walk) away.

Cashpoint machine
There are two cash machines in Gledhow Wing (where the neonatal unit is based) - one in the shop which is in the reception area of Gledhow Wing and one is in Costa Coffee which is situated in the end of the canteen area. There are other cash machines in other wings of the hospital if you need one, please speak to a member of staff.

Access to a midwife...
We do not have resident midwives on the neonatal unit but we can access the service in an emergency via the maternity assessment unit, outpatient clinic or the wards. Mum’s who are still in-patients have midwifery access 24 hours a day.

Interpreting services...
Interpreting services (for most languages) are available for all booked face-to-face meetings and can be arranged via telephone.

Laundry facilities...
There are no laundry facilities at St James’ however, we can provide emergency clothing packs for infants until families can purchase their own. Also, toiletry packs are available for parents in emergency situations.
