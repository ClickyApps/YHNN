Maps & Directions

How to access the unit...
Maternity entrance is at the rear of the hospital and is signposted from the main entrance. The SCBU buzzer is clearly marked prior to entry to the unit.

Nearest car park...
There is a multi storey car park on site which has a few reserved spaces for parents whose baby is in intensive care.

Car parking charges...
There are a few spaces close to the maternity entrance for use by parents of intensive care babies. Parents need to ask the nursing staff for a permit which allows them to park for free. Other car parking is in the multi-storey car park which is situated at the front of the hospital. This is also free for parents, they need to bring their car park ticket with them and validate it on the unit. All other visitors need to pay to park in the multi-storey car park.

Nearest bus stop...
Wiggington Road which is the main road just outside the hospital. There are several buses that pass the hospital, timetables available online via first bus company.

Nearest railway station...
York city centre is approximately 15 minute walk from the hospital or a short taxi or bus journey. There is a free taxi phone at the SCBU entrance.

Visiting Information

Parents...
Parents can visit at any time - day or night.

Number of visitors...
Maximum four visitors per cot.

Siblings...
Siblings cab visit at any time provided they are accompanied by a parent.

Grandparents...
Grandparents can visit between 14:00-15:30 and 18:30-20:00 hours unless baby has just been born and requires intensive care.

Family and friends...
Only two people (other than parents) are allowed at each visit.

Restrictions...
All visitors (except parents and siblings) must visit between 14:00-15:30 and 18:30-20:00 hours. No children under the age of 16 years are allowed unless they are siblings.

Hygiene and infection prevention...
All outdoor clothing must be removed before gaining entrance to the unit and hands must be washed in alcohol gel. General advice given when required by nursing staff to parents and visitors.

Ward Information

Mobile phone use...
Mobile phones must be kept on silent while on the unit.

Parent facilities...
Toilet, shower, kitchen sitting room available to all parents. Bedsits only for parents and babies prior to discharge.

Parent accommodation...
We have two bedsits on the unit and priority is given to parents who are preparing for the discharge of their baby.

Breastfeeding facilities and support...
Privacy screens are available when breastfeeding at the cot side. These are located in the corridor and can be collected by either the nursing staff or parents. An infant feeding co-ordinator works on the post natal ward Monday-Friday 09:00-17:00 and they can be contacted by the nursing staff if needed. In addition, all nursing staff are trained to BFI standards.

Facilities for siblings...
Toys and games available in the nurseries on SCBU and we have a sitting room with a television and a games console.

Parent support group...
There are coffee mornings run once a month by a community neonatal group and details are available from the neonatal community outreach nurses. The unit also has a special care support group and information can be found in the parent’s hand book or on facebook.

Religious and spiritual support...
Multidenominational support can be accessed at any time. There is a Chapel on the main corridor between junction 4 and 5. It can also be arranged on the ward at the patient’s bedside.

Play specialists...
There are no play specialists available at York Hospital.

Telephone and computer access...
There is no telephone or computer access provided by the hospital and parents will have to go off the unit to use their own mobile phones.

Bereavement support...
Pastoral bereavement office is situated on the main corridor between junction 6 and 7 (telephone number 01904 725392). All staff have access to multidenominational support networks and phone numbers.

Counselling...
Counselling information is available from nursing staff or parents can phone the Family Support Team on 0500 618140.

Other important stuff...
If parents require any further information just ask a member of staff.

Who’s who

Clinical lead...
Dr Guy Millman, Consultant Paediatrician. Telephone 01904 726005.

Nurse manager...
Ann Elliott, Ward Manager. Telephone 01904 726005.

Breastfeeding advisor...
All staff are trained to give advice and support to breast feeding mums.

Family centred care/developmental care lead...
The named nurse will care for your baby’s individual needs.

What to expect

Ward round...
A ward round takes please between 09:00 and 10:00 every morning.

Baby rest time...
Rest time is based on the individual needs of the baby.

Who to talk to about your baby’s care...
Please talk to the named nurse for your baby or the nurse-in-charge in the first instance. Medical staff can be called if necessary.

Talking to the medical team...
Parents can talk to the medical team during the ward round or they can request a private meeting which will be arranged by the named nurse.

Who to talk to if you have any concerns...
Please talk to the nurse who is caring for your baby, the ward sister, the sister in charge of the shift or a member of the medical staff. You can also ask to speak to your baby’s named consultant.

Patient Advocacy Liaison Service (PALS)...
The PALS office is located near the main entrance and is open Monday-Friday 09:00-17:00 or telephone 01904 726262.

Hospital Services

Catering/refreshment facilities...
Ellerby’s restaurant is situated on main corridor at junction 2 and there is a café and shops located near the main entrance. The parents kitchen on SCBU provides refreshments such as tea and coffee free of charge.

Other shops on site/nearby...
There is a Costa Coffee, a newsagents, a pharmacy and a gift shop in the main entrance of the hospital (which is within approximately 10 minutes walking distance to York city centre).

Cashpoint machine
The cashpoint machine is located near the main entrance.

Access to a midwife...
A midwife on the post natal ward is available for advice. Staff can arrange an appointment or mum can access this service herself.

Interpreting services...
Pearl Linguistics may be contacted by phone, face to face or written confirmation. Any interpreting service is usually arranged by staff.

Laundry facilities...
Parents should take their own baby’s clothes home to wash otherwise they will be laundered with the hospital items and may be lost. There are no facilities for parents to wash laundry in the unit.
